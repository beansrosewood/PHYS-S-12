Sketch of the "high/low" LED circuit:

On low beam the voltage across resistor R2 is 1.34vdc. Since V=IR,and I=V/R, therefore, 1.34vdc/1kohms= .00134 amps or 1.34mA is the amperage through both resistor R1 and the LED on low beam.

When the switch is pressed the voltage across R2 is 1.08vdc. Therefore, on high beam the amperage through R1 is: 1.08vdc/1kohms= .00108 amps or 1.08mA. However, the amperage through the LED would be higher due to the inclusion of R1 in the circuit when the switch is closed. The total resistance is: 1/R1 + 1/R2= 1/total R. So, 1/470ohms+1/1kohms=319ohms. Therefore, on high the LED current(which is the same as the total circuit current) is V/R= 1.08vdc(measured)/319ohms= .00339 amps or 3.39mA.

Voltage readings across the LED

shown on TinkerCAD.

This circuit changes the brightness of the LED by having two possible voltages across the LED terminals. The voltage is changed by closing the pushbutton switch. This created a second parallel resistor in the circuit which lowers the total resistance to ground for the LED, causing the voltage across the LED to increase from 1.9vdc on low beam to 2.1vdc on high beam. The low resistor(which is always in the circuit is a 1k ohms. The resistor that is engaged by the switch for high beam operation is 470 ohms.
